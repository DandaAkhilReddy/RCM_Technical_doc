Success Criteria: By end of 2025, reduce manual processes to 30%, achieve sub-5-day payment posting, and improve Days in AR to 30-35 days. Combined automation initiatives should deliver $600K+ in annual operational savings.

Critical Success Factors

1. Executive Sponsorship & Change Management

Strong C-suite commitment required for funding, priority-setting, and driving organizational adoption. Staff must be brought along through training, communication, and demonstrating benefits.

2. Sequenced Implementation

Q1-Q2 quick wins (bank integration, RPA) must deliver promised ROI to fund Q3-Q4 initiatives. Maintain quarterly review gates before advancing to next phase.

3. Vendor Partnership

Select technology partners with proven healthcare RCM experience. Require success-based payment terms where possible to align incentives.

4. Data Quality Foundation

Q1 data governance initiative is prerequisite for automation success. Garbage-in-garbage-out applies - automation amplifies data quality issues if not addressed first.

5. Staff Redeployment Strategy

Plan for redeployment of staff time freed by automation. Target higher-value activities like denial management, payer relations, and AR follow-up rather than headcount reduction.
