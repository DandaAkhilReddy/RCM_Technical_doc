Executive Overview

High-level summary of RCM system status, critical findings, and strategic recommendations

Executive Summary

Page Management Associates' Revenue Cycle Management system currently operates with 95% manual processes, creating significant operational inefficiencies and revenue leakage. The organization lacks automated bank integration, experiences 24-hour delays in clinical data synchronization, and manages reconciliation through manual Excel-based workflows.

This comprehensive technical analysis identifies $2.5M in annual revenue recovery potential through strategic automation initiatives. Priority recommendations include implementing bank integration, deploying RPA for insurance follow-up, and migrating to an HL7-compatible billing platform.

The proposed 2025 implementation roadmap requires an estimated investment of $250-350K across four quarters, with expected ROI achievement within 8-12 months through reduced manual labor, faster collections, and improved denial management.

Critical Finding: Manual Processes

95% of current operations are manual, including bank reconciliation, payment posting verification, and insurance follow-up tracking. This creates high labor costs and increased error rates.

System Integration Gaps

AdvancedMD lacks HL7 support, causing 24-hour ADT sync delays and preventing real-time clinical-to-billing data flow. No automated bank integration exists.

Revenue Recovery Opportunity

$2.5M annual revenue recovery identified through improved denial management, faster follow-up automation, and reduction of write-offs from aged AR.
